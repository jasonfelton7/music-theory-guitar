Basic Fretboard Knowledge

Triad Arpeggios

The diagrams below show the four main types of Triad Arpeggios. These are Major, Minor, Diminished and Augmented. Major arpeggios have a root, major 3rd and perfect 5th. Minor arpeggios have a root, minor 3rd and perfect 5th. Diminished arpeggios have a root, minor third and diminished 5th. Augmented arpeggios have a root, major 3rd and an augmented 5th. The root notes are shown circled in red.

Arpeggios

A chord is usually defined as a group of notes (typically three or more) sounded together, as a basis of harmony. Arpeggios are the notes of a chord played in sequence rather than at the same time. A seventh arpeggio consists of the same notes as seventh chords. (Consisting of a Root, 3rd, 5th and 7th).
