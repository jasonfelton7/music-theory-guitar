Basic Fretboard Knowledge

Relationship Between Major and Minor Scales

Every Major Scale has a relative natural minor scale that starts on the 6th degree. Conversely, every natural minor scale has a relative major scale that starts on the 3rd degree. The diagrams below demonstrate this. Notice that the C major scale and A natural minor scale contain all the same notes.

Modes

Each degree of the major scale is the root of a different mode. They use the same notes as the major scale, but each one sounds different by starting on a different note and therefore having different relationships between the notes because of that.

The modes of the major scale are as follows: Ionian (this is actually the Major Scale itself), Dorian, Phrygian, Lydian, Mixolydian, Aeolian (also known as Natural Minor) and Locrian.

Modes of the G Major Scale

The diagrams below show a possible fingering pattern for each of the seven modes of the major scale. The Ionian mode has a major 2nd, 3rd, 6th and 7th. It has a perfect 4th and perfect 5th. The other modes have notes that are altered from this pattern. The notes displayed in red are the root notes of each mode.
